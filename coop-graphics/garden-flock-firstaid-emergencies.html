Common Flock Emergencies: First Response

Initial steps while you assess whether a vet call is needed

Condition Signs First Response Call Vet If...
Wound / Injury Bleeding, torn skin, feather loss with raw skin Isolate, flush with saline, apply Vetericyn, bandage Deep puncture, body-cavity injury, shock
Bumblefoot Hard dark scab on foot pad, possible swelling Daily warm Epsom salt soaks, antibiotic ointment, soft bedding Lameness, deep infection, no improvement in 5-7 days
Egg Binding Straining, penguin posture, lethargy, abdominal swelling Warm steam, 20-min warm soak, oil at vent, quiet isolation No egg passed in 2-4 hours of supportive care
Crop Problem Crop hard/full at morning, or squishy with sour smell Withhold solid food, offer water, gentle crop massage for impaction Not resolved in 24 hrs, sour crop (needs antifungal)
Mites / Lice Restless, feather damage, tiny bugs at vent or under wings Poultry dust on birds, treat coop for mites; repeat in 10 days Anemia, severe infestation, repeated re-infestation
Prolapse Pink or red tissue visible at vent Isolate immediately, keep tissue moist with saline, darken pen Always -- prolapse is a medical emergency
Heat Stress Panting, wings out, pale comb, collapse Move to cool shade, cool water, wet feet and legs, electrolytes Collapse, seizures, no improvement within 30 minutes
