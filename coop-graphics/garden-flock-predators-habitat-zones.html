Priority Predator Risks by Santa Cruz County Habitat Zone

Your top threat varies by location. Source: CDFW; UC IPM; Bay Nature Santa Cruz Mountains wildlife monitoring; KQED.

Coastal Urban Santa Cruz City, Capitola, Aptos, Soquel

Top threats: Raccoon (high density along creek corridors), roof rat (dominant rat species in coastal areas), opossum (ubiquitous), Cooper's hawk (year-round in riparian corridors and parks).

Gray fox present in brushy margins. Mountain lion rare but CDFW documents corridors through even urban edges. Priority: raccoon-proof latch hardware and buried apron; rodent exclusion and metal feed storage.

San Lorenzo Valley Boulder Creek, Ben Lomond, Felton, Scotts Valley

Top threats: Mountain lion (CDFW 2023 alert; UC Davis Puma Project GPS-confirmed corridors); bobcat (active in chaparral and forest edges); gray fox (abundant in mixed forest and chaparral).

Long-tailed weasel likely more common here than coastal sightings suggest. Priority: dusk lockup is non-negotiable; structural exclusion for weasels; no outdoor overnight situations of any kind.

Pajaro Valley / Agricultural Watsonville, Corralitos, Freedom, La Selva Beach

Top threats: Coyote (high density due to rodent prey base in agricultural fields); red-tailed hawk (highest density in county in open agricultural land); Norway rat (more common in agricultural settings than coastal urban).

Uncovered pasture situations carry the greatest hawk predation risk of any zone in the county. Priority: covered runs for any free-range situation; coyote-proof fence with buried apron and outward overhang.

Coastal North County Davenport, Bonny Doon, Swanton

Top threats: Gray fox (chaparral and coastal scrub habitat); coyote (coastal terrace and ridgeline); long-tailed weasel (directly observed at Bonny Doon Beach, July 2020).

More rural and less urban-adapted predator community than coastal Santa Cruz City. Priority: hardware cloth exclusion at 1/2-inch or finer on all openings; raised coop floor to prevent weasel entry via rat tunnels.
