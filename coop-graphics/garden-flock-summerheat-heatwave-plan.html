Heat Wave Response Plan for Your Flock

Step-by-step actions before, during, and after an inland Santa Cruz County heat event

Day Before

  • ✓ Freeze water bottles or ice blocks
  • ✓ Prepare extra waterers, clean all
  • ✓ Add shade cloth or tarps if needed
  • ✓ Fill kiddie pool for waterfowl
  • ✓ Buy electrolytes if not on hand

Morning (Before 10 a.m.)

  • ✓ Put ice in all waterers
  • ✓ Open all coop vents fully
  • ✓ Offer frozen treats or wet greens
  • ✓ Set up fans if available
  • ✓ Do not feed high-energy scratch

Peak Heat (1 to 5 p.m.)

  • ✓ Check birds every 60 to 90 min
  • ✓ Refresh water as it warms
  • ✓ Mist birds lightly if distressed
  • ✓ Limit handling to emergencies
  • ✓ Keep waterfowl pool filled fresh

Source: UC ANR, Backyard Poultry Heat Management; University of Georgia Cooperative Extension, Poultry Heat Stress Management
