Best Garden Crops to Grow as Flock Treats

Nutrient value, how to grow it, and how to serve it in coastal Santa Cruz County

Crop Key Nutritional Benefit How to Grow Here How to Serve
Kale / Collards Vitamins A and K, calcium, carotenoids for yolk color Year-round in our climate; direct-sow or transplant Sept-Feb for main crop Hang whole plant in run; tear leaves into run; strip stalks
Pumpkin / Winter Squash Beta-carotene (vitamin A), seeds have mild antiparasitic properties Sow May-June; harvest Sept-Oct; stores for months Halve and place cut-side down in run; birds excavate seeds and flesh
Sunflowers (seed heads) 22% protein, healthy fats; excellent during fall molt Direct-sow Apr-May in full sun; harvest Aug-Sept; dry heads Offer whole dried heads; or hand-strip seeds as a high-protein supplement
Leaf Amaranth High protein for a leafy green; iron, calcium; seeds also nutritious Sow May-June after soil warms; fast-growing; drought-tolerant Harvest leaves throughout summer; toss whole plant when going to seed
Swiss Chard Vitamins A, C, K; offer in moderation (oxalic acid) Year-round; most productive Sept-May in coastal areas Handful of leaves 2-3x per week; not a daily staple
Peas (snap or shell) Protein, vitamins; vines and leaves also palatable Sow Oct-Feb for spring harvest; pull whole plant after harvest Fresh only; toss whole plants; never dried (digestive issues)
Herbs (oregano, parsley, thyme) Antimicrobial compounds; vitamins A and C; enrichment Perennial in our climate; grow in any corner of the treat bed Fresh sprigs tossed into run; or added to nest boxes
Comfrey (Russian) Up to 23% crude protein (dry weight); B12 precursors, calcium Plant once; perennial; cut 5-6 times per season; full sun to part shade Fresh leaves only; offer cut leaves directly; never dried
Cucumbers / Melons Hydration; cooling; low calorie; not a nutritional powerhouse Cucumbers: May-Aug in coastal areas; melons need warmer microclimate Halve and place in run on hot days; excellent hydration treat
Source: UC ANR Small Farms Program; UC ANR Poultry Nutrition; Cornell Cooperative Extension Poultry. Planting windows for USDA Zone 9b-10a, Santa Cruz County coast.
