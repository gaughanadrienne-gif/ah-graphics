Santa Cruz County Flock Predator: Kill Sign Diagnostic

Match the evidence you find to narrow down your suspect. Source: UC IPM; UMD Extension FS-1132; eXtension Poultry Predator Management.

Predator When What Is Missing or Taken Evidence Left Behind Entry Method
Raccoon Night Head; entrails; breast muscle Body near water; egg shells with lengthwise fractures; legs/feet missing if reach-through Reach through wire; climb; dig; opens simple latches
Gray Fox Night Whole bird (cached away) Minimal feathers at site; scat on elevated surfaces Digs under; climbs (the only canid that climbs)
Coyote Night / Dusk Whole bird carried away Scattered feathers; clean neck bite; no consumption at scene Digs under; jumps fences up to 6 ft; forces gaps
Bobcat Dawn / Dusk / Any 1 to 2 birds; mostly consumed or cached Scratch marks on back and shoulders; scat left uncovered in open areas Jumps; forces open or weakly latched coop doors
Mountain Lion Primarily Night Large bird taken; rarely caches poultry (caching behavior primarily documented with large prey) Bite to back of neck; large tracks (2.75 to 4 in wide; large males occasionally larger) Forces any unsecured closure; follows deer to yards
Great Horned Owl Night / Dawn Head and neck eaten; small birds taken whole Decapitated carcass; large talon puncture marks; body otherwise intact Unsecured vent openings; open upper windows in coop
Cooper's Hawk Day Small to medium bird (up to 1.5 lbs) Breast cleanly plucked; feathers at strike site or in nearby tree Aerial ambush in open run or free-range area
Red-tailed Hawk Day Young or bantam birds Clean talon wounds; breast plucked; feathers under nearby elevated perch Aerial strike on birds in uncovered area
Long-tailed Weasel Night / Any Nothing (birds cached in corners) Multiple nearly-decapitated carcasses throughout coop; tiny 1/2-inch tracks Holes as small as 1 inch; rat tunnels under floor
Opossum Night Eggs; chicks; internal organs Crushed egg shells; torn underbelly starting at cloaca; typically 1 bird Climbs; reaches through wire; slow but persistent
Rats Night Eggs; young chicks; bites on toes of roosting adults Egg shell fragments nearby; droppings near feeder; gnaw marks on wood Gaps 1/4 inch or larger; burrow from below; climb at upper levels
Domestic Dog Day Multiple birds killed; none consumed Ragged wounds across whole body; carcasses scattered; claw-mark paw prints Forces through weak fencing; pursues free-range birds in yard
