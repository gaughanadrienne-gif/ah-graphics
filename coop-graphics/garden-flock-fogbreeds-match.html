Which Fog Belt Breed Matches Your Situation?

A quick-reference guide by keeper type and priority

Best All-Around
Black Australorp
Highest consistent production, calm temperament, quiet, moderate single comb. The right starting point for most new fog-belt keepers.
Urban Lots / Neighbors Nearby
Wyandotte or Easter Egger
Both are reliably quiet. Wyandotte's rose comb is the best structural choice. Easter Egger's pea comb is also excellent.
Families with Young Children
Buff Orpington
The most consistently gentle breed. Tolerates handling, rarely pecks. Lower production than Australorps but worth it for calm handling.
Best Comb for the Fog Belt
Wyandotte or Dominique
Rose combs on both breeds provide the best moisture resistance of any common backyard breed available from major hatcheries.
Garden Integration
Speckled Sussex
Active, curious foragers that work with the garden rather than against it. Good pest scratchers without being destructively aggressive.
Heritage and Conservation
Dominique
America's oldest recognized breed, on the Livestock Conservancy Watch List. Rose comb, calm nature, and genuine fog-belt suitability.
