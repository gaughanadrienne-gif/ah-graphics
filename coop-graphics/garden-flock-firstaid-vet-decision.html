Treat at Home or Call the Vet?

A practical guide for California backyard flock keepers

Generally manageable at home

  • ✓Minor cuts and abrasions (no deep tissue)
  • ✓Mild bumblefoot (no deep infection, bird walking)
  • ✓Broken blood feather (remove or apply styptic)
  • ✓Mild external parasites (mites or lice, early)
  • ✓Mild heat stress (bird still alert, standing)
  • ✓Normal molt (feather loss with new pin feathers)
  • ✓Slight decrease in egg production (seasonal)

Call an avian or exotic vet

  • ✗Any respiratory symptoms (rattling, gasping, nasal discharge)
  • ✗Egg binding not resolved in 2-4 hours
  • ✗Any prolapse of vent tissue
  • ✗Deep puncture wounds or predator attack injuries
  • ✗Neurological signs (circling, head tilt, tremors)
  • ✗Sour crop or impacted crop not improving in 24 hours
  • ✗Multiple sick birds or suspected contagious illness
  • ✗Collapse, inability to stand, unresponsive bird

When in doubt, call. A 5-minute phone conversation with a poultry-savvy vet or avian clinic can save your bird's life and give you confidence in your next steps. Most practices will answer a basic triage question over the phone.
