Flock Treats: Safe vs. Avoid Quick-Reference

Keep treats to 10% of daily diet. Balanced feed is the foundation.

Safe to Grow and Serve
+ Kale, collards, chard Offer freely; excellent nutrition
+ Pumpkin and squash Flesh and seeds both safe; serve fresh
+ Sunflower seeds High protein; ideal during molt
+ Comfrey leaves (fresh) Very high protein; cut-and-come-again
+ Leaf amaranth High protein for a leafy green; fast-growing
+ Fresh peas (snap or shell) Offer fresh only; never dried
+ Cucumbers and melons Hydration and cooling on hot days
+ Oregano, parsley, thyme Antimicrobial and nutrient benefits
+ Ripe tomatoes Safe; avoid all green parts of the plant
+ Watermelon rinds and flesh Excellent summer treat for all species
Never Feed These
X Avocado (any part) Persin is toxic; can be fatal to all poultry species
X Raw or dried beans Phytohaemagglutinin toxin; potentially fatal
X Rhubarb leaves Oxalic acid and glycosides; can cause kidney damage
X Tomato and pepper plant foliage Solanine in all nightshade plant parts (not ripe fruit)
X Green potato skins Solanine; cooked ripe potato flesh is safe
X Moldy or rotten produce Mycotoxins are harmful to all poultry; when in doubt, compost it
X Onions (large amounts) Hemolytic anemia risk; small amounts unlikely to harm
X Chocolate Theobromine is toxic to birds; never offer any amount
X Dried comfrey Alkaloid content concentrates; fresh leaves only
X Salty or heavily processed food Salt toxicity in poultry; no nutritional value
Sources: ASPCA Animal Poison Control; UC Davis Veterinary Medicine; UC IPM; Cornell Cooperative Extension Poultry.
