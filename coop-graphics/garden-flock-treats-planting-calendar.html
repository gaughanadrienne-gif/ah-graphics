Flock Treat Garden: Season-by-Season Planting Calendar

Santa Cruz County coastal climate (USDA Zone 9b-10a). Inland pockets run 2-4 weeks warmer.

FALL (September - November)
Month Sow / Plant Now Harvest / Serve Now
September
Start kale, collards, chard transplants; sow amaranth final succession
Sunflower heads (dry and serve); summer squash and cucumbers still producing
October
Sow sugar snap peas; transplant brassica starts to main bed
Pumpkins and winter squash (store for winter); comfrey final cut of season
November
Continue pea sowing; plant Russian comfrey roots (fall planting establishes well)
Kale, chard, collards in production; stored pumpkins; dried sunflower seeds
WINTER (December - February)
Month Sow / Plant Now Harvest / Serve Now
December
Sow overwintering peas; plant parsley and thyme starts
Kale, chard, collards; stored winter squash; herb sprigs
January
Sow peas for spring; plan summer crops (order sunflower seeds)
Kale, chard; pea vines emerging (great for ducks and geese)
February
Start pumpkin and sunflower seeds indoors late Feb for transplant in May
Kale, chard; snap peas beginning to produce; comfrey returning
SPRING (March - May)
Month Sow / Plant Now Harvest / Serve Now
March
Direct-sow sunflowers in full-sun spot; sow amaranth in warmest area
Peak pea harvest; kale bolting (flowers are nutritious); comfrey first cuts
April
Sow cucumbers under cover; transplant pumpkin starts outdoors after mid-month
Peas, comfrey, new kale growth; pull spent pea plants and toss to flock
May
Transplant pumpkins and sunflowers; direct-sow amaranth and summer squash
Comfrey at peak production; amaranth seedling thinnings; herb cuttings
SUMMER (June - August)
Month Sow / Plant Now Harvest / Serve Now
June
Succession-sow amaranth and cucumbers; sow pumpkins in hot inland spots
Cucumbers, comfrey, amaranth leaves; early summer squash
July
Sow kale and chard for fall (start now for Sept harvest); sow winter squash
Cucumbers, amaranth, comfrey; summer squash; sunflower heads approaching maturity
August
Transplant kale and chard starts; sow peas for early fall (in coastal areas)
Sunflower heads maturing (begin drying); amaranth going to seed; cucumbers, squash
Source: UC ANR Vegetable Planting Guide for Santa Cruz County; UC Cooperative Extension Monterey Bay Area. Planting windows for coastal Zone 9b-10a; inland areas of the county run 2-4 weeks warmer in summer and fall.
