Breed Heat Tolerance for Santa Cruz County Flocks

Chicken breeds grouped by heat tolerance and suitability for coastal vs. inland summers

Breed Heat Tolerance Best For Notes
Leghorn (White) High Coastal and inland Mediterranean breed; light body, large single comb for heat release
Ancona High Coastal and inland Mediterranean origin; active forager, excellent layer
Minorca High Coastal and inland Large comb and wattles facilitate heat dissipation
Rhode Island Red Medium Coastal, manage inland Hardy dual-purpose; heavier body requires extra shade and water in heat
Easter Egger / Ameraucana Medium Coastal, manage inland Pea combs reduce heat release; provide extra shade in hot spells
Buff Orpington Lower Fog belt preferred Dense feathering holds heat; requires robust shade, cool water, and airflow
Brahma Lower Fog belt preferred Very large, feather-footed; vulnerable in temperatures above 85 degrees
Silkie Lower Fog belt preferred Fluffy feathers trap heat; walnut comb limits heat dissipation; monitor closely inland

Source: UC ANR Poultry Resources; American Poultry Association breed standards. Comb size is a primary factor in avian thermoregulation. Larger combs increase heat dissipation surface area.
