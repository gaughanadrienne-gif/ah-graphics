Chickens vs. Ducks: Side-by-Side for Santa Cruz County

Key decision factors for a coastal California backyard flock

Factor Chickens Ducks
Eggs per year 180 to 300 (breed-dependent) 300 to 340 (Khaki Campbell)
Winter laying Drops significantly with short days More consistent year-round
Egg flavor Mild, familiar Rich, fuller flavor; excellent for baking
Space needed 4 sq ft coop + 10 sq ft run per bird (UC ANR) Similar floor space; no roosts needed
Water management Standard waterers; easy Need deep water daily; creates mud
Mess level Scratches and dusts; manageable Mud and wet droppings; significant in our wet winters
Slug and snail control Eats some; not systematic Outstanding; best natural slug control available
Garden bed safety Scratches destructively Bills gently; less plant damage
Noise Hens cluck; roosters crow loudly Hens quack; drakes nearly silent
Neighbor impact Manageable without a rooster Generally lower; ducks less shrill
Predator vulnerability Vulnerable; need secure housing Also vulnerable; especially to raccoons at water sources
Coastal fog tolerance Good; avoid prolonged damp housing Excellent; bred for wet conditions
Temperament Variable by breed; some very friendly Often calmer; less flighty than chickens
Startup cost Lower; coops and equipment widely available Similar birds; add water management infrastructure
Source: UC ANR Poultry Resources; American Livestock Breeds Conservancy | ambitiousharvest.com
