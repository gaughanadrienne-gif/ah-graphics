# UGC Portfolio — Sample Video Scripts & Shot Lists

Five videos, five different formats, all shot on your phone with products you already own.
The goal is range: a brand should look at the portfolio and see you can do *their* brief.

**Order to shoot them:** 1 → 2 → 4 → 3 → 5. The first two are the easiest wins; #5 needs
harvest footage so start collecting b-roll for it now.

---

## Universal shooting rules (apply to every video)

- **Vertical, 4K, 30fps.** Wipe the lens every time — it's the #1 amateur tell.
- **Natural light only.** Best windows: first 2 hours after sunrise or last 2 before sunset.
  Foggy-diffuse midday light on the coast also works — it's basically a free softbox.
- **Shoot 3 hook variations per video.** The first 1.5 seconds is 80% of performance;
  brands love getting hook options and it justifies your hook add-on pricing later.
- **Every clip 5–10 seconds longer than you think you need.** Editing room to breathe.
- **Hands and product sharp, background soft.** Tap-to-focus on the product.
- **Audio:** talk 6–12 inches from the phone mic, or record voiceover separately in a
  closet/car afterward. Wind is the enemy; block it with your body.
- **B-roll library to build as you go** (10–15s clips, reusable across all videos):
  watering close-up, hands in soil, seedling trays, harvest into a basket/bowl,
  chickens ranging, fog rolling over the garden, gate/coop door opening, tool bucket.

## Video 1 — Product Demo: "5 Jobs, One Tool"

**Format:** Talking-head open → hands-only demo cuts. **Length:** 45–60s.
**Product:** Your most-used tool (hori hori, pruners, or hand cultivator).
**What it proves to brands:** You can demo a physical product clearly and fast.

| # | Shot | Say | Show |
|---|------|-----|------|
| 1 | Selfie, tool held to camera | **Hook:** "If I could only keep one tool in my garden, it's this one — here's why." | Face + tool, garden behind you |
| 2 | Hands-only, ground level | "One: transplanting. The depth markings mean I never guess." | Digging a transplant hole, sliding a seedling in |
| 3 | Hands-only | "Two: weeds with taproots come out whole, not snapped." | Levering out a full weed, root and all |
| 4 | Hands-only | "Three: it opens every soil bag and cuts every twine." | Slicing a bag top / cutting twine |
| 5 | Hands-only | "Four: dividing perennials without dragging out a shovel." | Splitting a clump |
| 6 | Hands-only | "Five: harvesting root crops without stabbing them." | Loosening soil next to a carrot/beet, pulling it |
| 7 | Selfie close | **CTA:** "Three years, zero rust, still sharp. Link's where you'd expect it." | Tool tap on palm, smile |

**Hook variations to also shoot:** (a) "Stop buying garden tool sets. You need one tool."
(b) Silent hook: stab tool into soil in front of lens, then look up.

## Video 2 — Unboxing + First Impressions: Seed Order

**Format:** Talking-head + tabletop overhead. **Length:** 30–45s.
**Product:** A recent seed order (or re-pack one you have — nobody checks the tape).
**What it proves to brands:** Authentic reaction energy + you can make packaging a moment.

| # | Shot | Say | Show |
|---|------|-----|------|
| 1 | Overhead, package on potting bench | **Hook:** "My fall garden just arrived in the mail — let's see if I over-ordered again." | Hands opening the package |
| 2 | Overhead | "Okay — germination rates printed right on the packet. Respect." | Flip packets, point at the detail |
| 3 | Selfie, packet held up | "This one's the gamble: [variety]. Nobody grows it on the coast, so obviously I'm going to." | Your genuine pick-of-the-order |
| 4 | Overhead | "Everything sorted by sowing date, because September-me will not remember." | Sorting packets into rows |
| 5 | Selfie | **CTA:** "Full review when these hit the soil in 3 weeks. Follow for the germination report." | Packets fanned like playing cards |

**Note:** The "gamble variety" beat is the whole video — that's the honest-reviewer
credibility brands are buying. Never skip it.

## Video 3 — Problem → Solution: Predator-Proofing the Coop

**Format:** Story-driven, mixed selfie + b-roll. **Length:** 50–60s.
**Product:** Hardware cloth, an auto door, or a latch — whatever your coop actually uses.
**What it proves to brands:** You can sell through a story, and you own the backyard-poultry niche.

| # | Shot | Say | Show |
|---|------|-----|------|
| 1 | Selfie, walking toward coop | **Hook:** "A raccoon can open a latch a toddler can open. I learned that the hard way." | Urgent walk-and-talk |
| 2 | B-roll | "Everything online said chicken wire was fine. Chicken wire keeps chickens *in*. It does not keep predators *out*." | Push finger through chicken wire flexing |
| 3 | Hands-only | "This is half-inch hardware cloth. Raccoon-proof, rat-proof, and it goes 12 inches into the ground for diggers." | Fist pressing on rigid mesh, pan down the buried apron |
| 4 | Hands-only | "And the latch situation: two-step, carabiner-backed. Raccoons can't do sequences." | Working the latch mechanism slowly |
| 5 | B-roll golden hour | "Two years, zero losses. The girls sleep fine and so do I." | Hens filing into the coop at dusk |
| 6 | Selfie | **CTA:** "Full predator-proofing checklist is free on my site — grab it before you build." | Point down / link gesture |

**Note:** CTA plugs your own Predator-Proof Coop Checklist — in the *sample* version this
cross-sells your lead magnet; in the *paid* version the brand's CTA replaces it. Show
brands both cuts; it demonstrates you understand CTA swaps.

## Video 4 — Routine Integration: "The 90-Minute Weekend Garden"

**Format:** Voiceover over b-roll, NO talking head. **Length:** 30–40s.
**Product:** Drip timer, watering wand, or granular fertilizer — something used *within* a routine.
**What it proves to brands:** You can do the aesthetic voiceover format (huge on Reels)
and integrate product without it feeling like an ad.

**Voiceover script (record separately, calm and unhurried):**

> "People think a food garden takes all weekend. Mine takes ninety minutes.
> Saturday morning: ten minutes walking with coffee, just looking — that's the real work.
> Twenty minutes harvesting whatever's ready.
> Thirty on the one big job of the week.
> The watering runs itself — I set the timer in March and haven't thought about it since.
> Last thirty: feeding, deadheading, done by nine a.m.
> Ninety minutes. Fog's not even burned off yet."

**Shot list (match beats in order):** coffee mug + garden walk POV → hands harvesting into
bowl → the "one big job" (whatever's true that week) → **product beat: hands adjusting the
timer, water starting in the drip line (hold 4 full seconds — this is the paid shot)** →
feeding/deadheading hands → wide of the garden in fog, exhale shot.

## Video 5 — Before/After Testimonial: Berry Feeding Results

**Format:** Results testimonial, selfie + harvest b-roll. **Length:** 40–50s.
**Product:** Whatever amendment/fertilizer you actually use on berries.
**What it proves to brands:** You can make results claims credibly — the highest-paying format.

| # | Shot | Say | Show |
|---|------|-----|------|
| 1 | Selfie, holding a full harvest bowl | **Hook:** "Last year this plant gave me a handful. This year, this — same plant. One change." | The bowl does the talking |
| 2 | B-roll / photo insert | "Last spring: leggy canes, small sour fruit. Classic coastal problem — fog, cool soil, slow feeding." | Old photo if you have one; struggling plant if not |
| 3 | Hands-only | "The change: feeding on a schedule instead of vibes. This, at green-up, at flower, and after first harvest." | Product in hand, scooping/applying at the base |
| 4 | B-roll | "Six weeks later the canes doubled. The bees found the flowers before I did." | Healthy canes, flowers, bees if you can get them |
| 5 | Hands close-up | "And now it's July and I'm picking every two days." | Slow-mo berry pick, the money shot |
| 6 | Selfie, eating one | **CTA:** "Exact feeding schedule is on my site. Your plant isn't the problem — the schedule is." | Genuine reaction |

**Honesty rule:** only claim what happened. If it was a different product or compost,
script the true version — brands doing due diligence will trust the portfolio *more*.

## After shooting: the portfolio checklist

1. Edit tight: cut every pause, caption everything (80% watch muted), keep native aspect 9:16.
2. Upload each as **unlisted YouTube**, grab `https://www.youtube.com/embed/VIDEO_ID`.
3. Paste embed URLs into `work-with-brands-data.json`, flip `comingSoon` to `false`, push.
4. Screen-record the finished page on mobile — that 15s screen recording *is* your DM pitch attachment.
